Introduction back to ToC

To make sense of the generated massive data in energy management, especially at the district scale, it is essential to resolve the cross-domain data interoperability problem and extract useful data and key information. Furthermore, the data are provided by different stakeholders; hence, it is vital to find a way to interchange it amongst the various stakeholders. The EM-KPI ontology is developed to exchange both the master data and key performance information between various stakeholders in districts and buildings, in order to enhance energy management on both scales, and to enable the exploitation of knowledge and insights for performance improvement.

The EM-KPI ontology contains two parts, namely the EM (energy management) master data part and the KPI (key performance indicator) part. The purpose of the KPI part is to represent the key performance information. It is supposed to describe the KPIs, including their definitions, associated performance goals and stakeholders, evaluated objects, calculations and required datum sources. This KPI part provides the basis for energy performance tracking and assessment. The purpose of the EM master data part is to integrate the master data in district and building EM. It is intended to describe the relationship between the master data domains, objects and elements. This part helps to exploit the knowledge and insights in the master data sources for identifying energy performance problems and key areas for improvement, and to help energy managers make informed decisions regarding energy efficiency measures. The master data domains in district and building energy management include energy domain (e.g., energy generation, energy supply, energy consumption), observation domain, building domain, facility domain, utility domain, occupancy, weather, location, etc.
